The Academy for Innovation and Entrepreneurship

The University of Maryland’s Academy for Innovation and Entrepreneurship is tasked by the University President and Provost to engage all 35,000 students with innovation and entrepreneurship

The Work

I facilitated and coached design thinking and lean start-up courses across multiple academic disciplines. The courses were experiential based- challenging students to step outside of their comfort zones and apply a human centered approach to projects they are passionate about.
Some courses I taught and coached in are How Do Innovators Think? (BMGT298B), Contemporary Issues in Entrepreneurship and Innovation (HEIP144), Innovation and Social Change: Do Good Now (HONR348D), Introduction to Design and Quality (ENES190H).

Additionally, outside of the classroom the Academy engaged students with design thinking in new and unique ways at events like the Social Enterprise Symposium, the First Look Fair, and TerpRide. (Picture below)

The Takeaways

Learned an array of human-centered design tools, stories, and methods and how to help others use them. I learned design thinking from leadership that hailed from Stanford’s d.school(LINK).

I was fortunate to work with a incredible team that was changing University of Maryland’s culture. On my way out, the Academy was brought I&E to 30% of of UMD students (LINK)

Athletics by Design

Founder and chair of Athletic Department Student Advisory Council

A strong culture is one most valuable assets any organizations can have. For colleges this is especially true- culture engages alumni, empowers students, and fosters a community.

I founded the Athletic Department Student Advisory Council to define and strengthen the University of Maryland’s culture.

The Need

Over three years I created a robust team of students from all disciplines on campus and developed strong relationships with the athletic department leadership. ADSAC came to represent the student body for the Athletic Department.

The Work

We applied design thinking to our mission. We conducted over fifty empathy interviews with undergraduate students, a defined specific point of view needs to design for, we created personas for needs often overlooked— for commuter students, freshman, and students who never attended athletic events.

The Takeaways

We implemented many changes over the years I led ADSAC, we engaged new students, defined rivalries, and started traditions. One of the largest changes was changing University policy to allow beer and alcohol to be sold at stadiums. This change gained national attention and with the available data has been successful in it’s mission in a safer and more enjoyable game day culture.

PrideShorts

I founded an apparel company to address an identified gap in the collegiate clothing market.

The Progress

I designed the product through customer interviews and countless design iterations. I pitched the business in several business competitions and raised $3,000 for my first run of inventory. I sourced a global manufacturer from Guangzhou, China and imported inventory to College Park, Maryland . I acquired trademark licenses and launched marketing initiatives. By my senior year I began developing prototypes for women’s lines and a men’s pant line.

The Takeaways

I was able to create a desirable product offering and a viable business model. Unfortunately, I was unable to scale the business due to collegiate licensing barriers. I learned more about business from PrideShorts than I did in my classes- I would absolutely do it again.
